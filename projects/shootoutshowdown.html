Shootout Showdown

This game is my first full length solo project that I am the most proud of. I put the most effort to date into it. This one was also made as a school project. The assignment was really simple. After we were given a tour of a video game museum we had to make an arcade game like the ones on the machines. A game that could be replayed over and over again. I chose a shooting gallery game inspired by Duck Hunt.

THE PROCESS

THE PROCESS I started off by making a gun system so that we had something to shoot with because a shooting gallery game without guns is just like walking through a museum. Making a gun was really simple. Whenever the player presses the left mouse button, we cast a ray from the eyes of the player to see if we hit something. If we did, we check if it's an enemy and if it is we deal damage.
Next up, because we're making a shooting gallery game we have to add some objects to shoot! I started out with mugs because a mug isn't that hard to model. After that was done I attached a script to it keeping track of health and that was that. Then I stumbled across my first challenge, I have to spawn in objects at random positions. The way I did this was by putting down a bunch of empty objects that only contain a position, I then added all of them to a list and then made a script that spawns them in at one of the random given locations. Lastly, I added a bullet limit because whenever you go to a fair and you see a shooting gallery stand, obviously you don't get unlimited shooting attempts. I decided that 100 bullets was enough and with that I had finished the first prototype of the game.
Now I had to add some variation in objects to prove I can actually 3D model because in order to get a good grade for this semester, I had to make and texture my own models to show that I am capable of making 3D art of my own. So I opened up blender and after about 9 continous hours of modelling I got this vase out of it:
This vase is the thing I put the most hours into. Especially texturing it. I wasn't quite sure what design to paint on it so I just drew a bunch of popular icons from pop culture. Stuff like the Batman logo, the Joker, Pepe the frog, you get the idea.
Now that I have finally made a unique model I had to add these to the pool of spawnable targets. I added in a golden mug which is worth 500 points for some variation and a slight dopamine rush.

LESSONS LEARNED

The most important thing I learned from this project has to do with art. Not every developer has access to a whole team of professional artists that can put hundreds of hours into a piece to be featured in game. This project essentially forced me to learn blender & 3D art and it was a very fun process. I got an art piece out of it that I am very proud of.

CONCLUSION

This is the most effort I EVER put into a project and I'm really proud of the final product. I do have plans to come back to this project and polish it further and eventually release it. With that said, I present to you Shootout Showdown.
